# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Online shoppers at the moment of purchase. Context: split attention, phone or desktop, often 11 pm at home. The product is already chosen; this interface is the closing instrument, not the sales pitch. Emotional goal: reassure, not excite.

### Brand Personality
Three concrete words: **crisp, architectural, unhurried**.

Voice: like a bank statement that takes itself seriously enough to be well-typeset. Not friendly-warm, not aggressively-minimal. Quiet confidence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial / Swiss-modern checkout. Think: a museum wall caption, a well-engraved legal document, an invoice typeset by someone who owned a Pantograph.

- **Fonts**: Bricolage Grotesque (variable opsz) for display, Manrope for body, Geist Mono for every number that touches currency. Explicitly not Inter / DM / Plus Jakarta / Instrument / IBM Plex / Space Grotesk / any Impeccable-reflex font.
- **Palette**: deep olive-bronze brand (OKLCH hue 85) — deliberately outside the blue/purple fintech monoculture. Neutrals are tinted toward the brand hue with chroma 0.006–0.012 for subconscious cohesion.
- **No**: pure black, pure white, gradients, drop shadows on cards, side-stripe borders, glowing accents, bouncy easing, round filled step dots.
- **Yes**: hairline 1px borders, flat 4px radius, tabular-nums everywhere numbers appear, editorial step markers (`— 01` instead of filled circles), generous vertical rhythm (24/32/48 scale).

### Anti-references
- Stripe default indigo
- Linear / Vercel monochrome
- Every AI-generated checkout from 2024–2025 (rounded-lg cards, soft shadows, Inter, indigo gradient on the pay button, blue-on-dark trust badges)

### Design Principles
1. **Type does the work, not decoration.** Hierarchy comes from weight, scale, and family contrast — never from colored pills, icon chips, or drop shadows.
2. **Money is typeset in mono with tabular lining figures.** Every price, total, and currency code is Geist Mono. Receipts are supposed to line up.
3. **Olive-bronze is the one accent.** It appears on brand elements, step markers, selection states, and CTA. Nowhere else. Per the 60-30-10 rule: accent is rare so it's loud.
4. **No containers without a reason.** Sections are separated by hairline dividers and generous whitespace, not nested card-in-card shells.
5. **4px radius, hairline borders, 100ms transitions.** Nothing is pill-rounded. Nothing has a soft shadow. Nothing bounces.
