# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Indie-brand shoppers at the moment of purchase. Context: scrolling-driven mood, often mobile in café daylight, often buying candles / sauces / zines / small-batch anything. Emotional goal: reward the curiosity that brought them here — make the checkout feel handmade, not corporate.

### Brand Personality
Three concrete words: **sunlit, confident, zine-made**.

Voice: like a risograph-printed menu at a good coffee shop. Warm, playful, never twee. Uses thick rules and tomato-red stamps for emphasis.

### Aesthetic Direction
Sun-bleached zine / risograph checkout. Think: an indie food brand's direct-mail box, a zine spread, a farmers-market sign-painted-by-hand.

- **Fonts**: Clash Display (or Gambarino, Migra Italic, PP Neue Machina) for headlines, Switzer or Geist for body, Redaction Mono or JetBrains Mono for numerics. Explicitly not Inter / Manrope / Space Grotesk default loadouts.
- **Palette**: sun-bleached cream `#F6EFD9` page, warm off-white `#FDF8E7` card, tomato-red stamp `#D64535` as primary accent, cobalt ink `#2E4BD2` for secondary marks, charcoal `#1A1915` for type. Three accents (tomato primary, cobalt secondary, olive `#727339` tertiary) — more color than editorial/luxe because zines thrive on controlled chaos.
- **No**: cold gradients, pure-white backgrounds, glossy drop shadows, round pill CTAs, indigo/purple fintech palette, sterile neutrals.
- **Yes**: thick 2–3px rules, stamped tomato tags, handset quote-marks ("") in display, slightly misaligned rules (risograph imperfection), generous margins, bold mono numerals.

### Anti-references
- Stripe, Linear, Vercel minimalism
- Every DTC wellness pastel serif
- "Brutalist but still Inter" AI-generated pages
- Over-saturated CMYK print-shop kitsch

### Design Principles
1. **Rules do the structural work.** Thick tomato and cobalt horizontals carry the layout; cards are rare.
2. **Color tells the hierarchy.** Tomato for CTA + section stamps; cobalt for secondary labels + links; olive for tertiary ornament. Never all three on the same chip.
3. **Headlines are stamped, not floated.** Serif-italic or chunky sans on a tomato stamp, angled ±1°, uppercase.
4. **Mono is for every number AND for eyebrow labels** — the zine typewriter aesthetic.
5. **2px radius (near-square), 2–3px rules, 100ms transitions.** No soft shadows, but a crisp offset `stamp` shadow (`3px 3px 0 currentColor`) is allowed on accent tags only.
